## What Is Working

1. **Capability framed as operational continuity wins.** The Apr 20 capacity post outperformed because it did not just say "we have machines"; it tied process, control, traceability, and production discipline to buyer predictability.
2. **Supplier-risk language creates high-intent engagement.** The Apr 24 OEM kit integration post has low early impressions but the highest CTR and engagement density. The handoff/rastreabilidade/audit-risk frame is worth repeating.
3. **Concrete engineering choices beat broad education.** The membrane fixation post did well because it names a real product decision: pins vs screws. This should become a repeatable "decision tradeoff" format.
4. **Metrology alone needs sharper pain.** The metrology and validation posts are technically solid, but CTR is lower. Next versions should lead with receiving inspection failures, NC risk, scrap, lot release delay, or auditor evidence gaps.
5. **Comments remain the main weakness.** The period still has 0 comments and 0 reposts in the current top table. The content is earning clicks and reactions, but not discussion.

## Recommended Next Content Moves

- Publish 2 more variants of the Apr 20 theme: "capacidade produtiva = previsibilidade de lote" and "quem consegue fabricar todos os dias?" with different hooks for operations, supply chain, and quality.
- Turn the Apr 24 OEM kit integration post into a 5-7 slide carousel. The single-image/text version has high-intent density but needs more reach.
- Create a recurring "decisao de engenharia" series: pinos vs parafusos, importado vs local, inspecao final vs controle em processo, fornecedor unico vs cadeia fragmentada.
- Reframe metrology posts with a cost/risk hook first: "o problema nao e medir no fim; e descobrir tarde demais", "recebimento rejeitado por evidencia fraca", or "NC que nasceu antes da CMM".
- Add stronger comment mechanics: ask a forced-choice technical question, e.g. "onde o risco aparece primeiro: desenho, processo, medicao ou recebimento?"
