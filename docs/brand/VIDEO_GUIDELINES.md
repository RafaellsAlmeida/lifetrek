# Lifetrek Medical Video Guidelines
**Version 1.0 | Video Production Standard**

These guidelines ensure that all video content—from institutional films to technical showcases—reflects Lifetrek’s commitment to precision, quality, and engineering excellence.

---

## 1. Video Style & Aesthetics
The visual style should be **Cinematic Industrial**. It must feel premium, clean, and highly technical.

- **Color Grading**: Natural tones with a slight cool (blue/teal) bias in shadows to align with Corporate Blue (`#004F8F`). Avoid "warm/yellow" commercial filters.
- **Lighting**: Bright, clinical, and clean. Use high-key lighting for laboratory/cleanroom settings and dramatic, focused lighting for CNC machining shots to highlight textures and precision.
- **Framing**:
  - **Macro/Close-ups**: Essential for showing the precision of medical components.
  - **Wide Architectural**: To show the scale and cleanliness of the facility.
  - **The Human Element**: Focus on technicians in full PPE (Class 7 Cleanroom protocols) to emphasize rigor and certification.

---

## 2. Motion Graphics & Typography
All text overlays must follow the brand’s typography and glassmorphism rules.

- **Primary Font**: Inter (Bold/Extra Bold for headlines, Regular for body).
- **Text Boxes**: Use the **Glassmorphism Card** style (Template A from Social Media Guru).
  - Background: Semi-transparent dark blue/black.
  - Blur: 15px - 20px.
  - Border: 1px steady white with low opacity.
- **Lower Thirds**: Clean, left-aligned, using Inter Bold for names and Inter Regular for titles.
- **Transitions**: Smooth cuts or subtle "fade to black/blue". Avoid flashy or "digital glitch" transitions.

---

## 3. B-Roll Categories
A balanced institutional video should include:

| Category | Subject Matter |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Precision** | Macro shots of Zeiss CMM probes, Citizen CNC lathes, and finished implants. |
| **Rigor** | Cleanroom entry protocols, gowning, and air shower sequences. |
| **Scale** | Drone shots or wide pans of the production floor and facility exterior. |
| **Partnership** | Collaborative sequences of engineers reviewing technical drawings on screen. |

---

## 4. Audio & Music
The soundscape should be **Modern & Minimalist**.

- **Music Style**: Professional, ambient, and steady. Avoid "upbeat corporate" or "ukulele" tracks. Think "Architectural" or "Tech-Ambient".
- **Sound Design**: Subtle foley (the sound of CNC machines, air systems) can be used to add realism, but should never overbear the music or voiceover.
- **Voiceover (VO)**:
  - **Tone**: Calm, authoritative, and expert (Portuguese-BR for local, English for international).
  - **Gender**: Neutral or Low-pitch (masculine/feminine) to convey stability and experience.

---

## 5. Institutional Video Brief (The Narrative Arc)
Every institutional video should follow this 5-stage structure:

1. **The Hook (0-10s)**: A high-impact macro shot of a complex component + "Precision is not an option; it's our foundation."
2. **The Problem/Context (10-25s)**: The complexity of medical regulations and the need for reliable local partners.
3. **The Solution (25-50s)**: Showcase of technology (CNC Swiss, Zeiss CMM) and the Class 7 Cleanroom.
4. **The Proof (50-70s)**: ISO 13485, ANVISA certification, and a mention of market leaders we serve.
5. **The Partnership (70-90s)**: A message about shared growth and "Engineering Excellence for Healthcare Innovation."

---

## 6. Technical Specifications
- **Resolution**: 4K (3840x2160) minimum for masters.
- **Aspect Ratio**: 16:9 for YouTube/Presentations; 9:16 for Social Highlights.
- **Frame Rate**: 24fps (Cinematic) or 60fps (for smooth slow-motion machining).
